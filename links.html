Here's a collection of links I think is worth sharing

No Meetings, No Deadlines, No Full-Time Employees - Sahil Lavingia
On how Gumroad runs their business. Truly interesting to see how different some companies think about work.
Understanding is a poor substitute for convexity (Antifragility) - Nassim Taleb (PDF-version)
This article by Nassim Taleb is a great introduction to Antifragility.
Shape Up - Basecamp (PDF version)
A book about how Basecamp rethinks product development
Open Source Polices - Basecamp
Great starting point to create your own privacy policies and terms of service.
Roam Research
Great notetaking tool. Made to mimick your brains neural network. "For connected thought"
The great books program - Tommy Collison
Want to read more of the old classics? Follow along Tommy Collisons project to read the old great books.
Be a Schedule Builder, Not a To-Do List Maker - Nir Eyal
An article about the value of scheduling your work and basing your work on your todo-list. It has worked wonders for me. (like, who doesn't use their calendar?)
Structured Procrastination - John Perry
Fantastic essay about procrastination. "...anyone can do any amount of work, provided it isn't the work he is supposed to be doing at that moment."
Islands and Bridges - David Perell
A great essay of how to connect ideas you still aren't sure how they are connected. Works for anything, not just writing.
